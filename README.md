# Maven Market Analysis

(Sales, Products, Returns & Customers | 1997–1998)

## Overview

This project presents a retail business performance analysis of the Maven Market dataset for the period 1997–1998. The analysis evaluates sales trends, product profitability, return behavior, customer value, and store performance to identify growth drivers, problem areas, and optimization opportunities.
The focus is on business insights and decision support, using interactive dashboards to clearly communicate performance and recommendations.

## Objectives

The key objectives of this analysis are to:
* Evaluate overall revenue, profit, transactions, and growth trends
* Analyze product performance and profitability
* Assess return behavior and its impact on revenue
* Understand customer demographics and purchasing behavior
* Compare store and regional performance
* Support data-driven business decisions

## Dashboards Covered

The analysis is structured into the following dashboards:
 ** Executive Sales Overview ** – Overall revenue, profit, growth trends, and regional contribution
 ** Product Analysis Dashboard  ** – Product-level revenue, profit, margins, and return patterns
 ** Return Analysis Dashboard ** – Return rate, revenue loss, and high-risk products/regions
 **Customer & Store Performance Dashboard ** – Customer value, income segments, store formats, and location performance

## Dataset Summary

 **Time Period **: 1997–1998
 **Total Revenue **: $1.76M
 **Net Revenue **: $1.75M
 **Total Profit **: $1.05M
 **Profit Margin **: ~60%
 **Total Transactions **: ~270K
 **Return Rate **: ~1%
 **Revenue Lost to Returns **: ~$17K
 **Total Customers **: 8,842
 **Revenue per Customer **: $199.56
 **Total Products **: 1,560
 **Total Quantity Sold **: ~833K units

## Tools Used

* Power BI – Data modeling, DAX measures, KPIs, and interactive dashboard creation
* Jupyter Notebook – Used for date format conversion and minor preprocessing

## Key Insights
### Executive Sales Overview

* Strong overall business performance with high profitability
* Revenue shows a steady upward growth trend
* North West region is a major contributor to revenue and profit
* Sales are concentrated in a limited number of stores and categories
* Low return rate helps minimize revenue loss

### Product Performance

* A small number of products generate a large share of revenue and profit
* High-ticket transactions significantly contribute to profitability
* Some products show high sales volume but lower margins
* Certain high-performing products also have higher return rates, indicating quality or expectation gaps

### Returns Analysis

* Overall return rate is low, but returns still have a measurable financial impact
* High-selling products tend to carry higher return risk
* Return quantities increase along with sales growth
* Some regions contribute more heavily to revenue loss due to returns

### Customer & Store Insights

* Average and low-income customers contribute most of the revenue
* Revenue contribution is nearly equal across genders
* Supermarkets and Deluxe Supermarkets outperform smaller store formats
* Revenue per customer indicates strong customer value
* Store performance varies significantly by location

## Findings

* Business growth is driven by product, store, and regional concentration
* Customer income level plays a major role in purchasing behavior
* Returns, while limited, require active monitoring and control
* Execution quality at the store level strongly influences performance

## Recommendations

* Focus on top-performing products while improving low-margin SKUs
* Target high-value customer segments to increase repeat purchases
* Optimize store operations, especially in underperforming locations
* Reduce returns through product quality checks and expectation management
* Apply regional strategies based on performance contribution
* Manage risk associated with high-ticket products

## Conclusion

Maven Market demonstrates a strong and profitable retail operation with consistent revenue growth and efficient cost management. While sales are driven by a concentrated set of products, stores, and regions, opportunities exist to improve profitability through better return management, operational optimization, and targeted customer strategies.

This analysis highlights how data-driven insights can support smarter decisions and sustainable business growth.
